Explanation

In this assigment, annual data is imported from WRDS with variables of Cash Flow, Earnings, Current Debt, Long-term Debt, Total Asset, Industry Classification, etc. All missing values are removed and all companies with 0 total asset are also removed. Summaries of data are shown below as well as the distribution and box plots.

Method

Market Leverage Ratio is computed by dividing sum of Current Debt and Long-term Debt by Total Asset. Book Leverage Ratio is computed by dividing sum of Current Debt and Long-term Debt by sum of Current Debt and Long-term Debt and Common Equity. The two histograms is ploted for each stocks in the market. The other plots are drawed by Industry Classifications. The boxplot shows all the available data of both Market and Book Leverage Ratio while the histogram of Market Leverage Ratio only shows ratio smaller than 2 because if we include the observations of very high ratios in the histogram, we cannot find much information in the graph.

Statistical Conclusion

By comparing the Market Leverage Ratio and the Book Leverage Ratio, we can tell that some of the Market Ratios is much higher than the Book Ratios. This is becasue sometimes market values are higher than book values. The boxplot of the Market Leverage Ratio shows that some of the companies are extremely highly leveraged by the market values. When we look at the mean Market and Book Leverage ratio for each industry, it is easy to find that Industry with index 55 and 60 have the highest leverage ratio. These two industries are Real Estate and Utilities. Industry with index 45 has the lowest leverage ratio and this industry is the Information Technology industry. When we look at the mean Earnings of each industry, Communication Services industry (index 50) has the highest earnings while the Real Estate industry (index 60) has the lowest earning.

Computer Code 

data2 = read.csv("data3.csv")
data2 = data2[complete.cases(data2),]
data2.noNA = data2[which(data2$at != 0),]

MLR2 = (abs(data2.noNA$dlc)+abs(data2.noNA$dltt))/abs(data2.noNA$at)
BLR2 = (abs(data2.noNA$dlc)+abs(data2.noNA$dltt))/(abs(data2.noNA$dlc)+abs(data2.noNA$dltt)+abs(data2.noNA$ceq))

hist(MLR2,breaks = 5000, xlim = c(0,2), main = "Market Leverage Ratio")
hist(BLR2,breaks = 200, main = "Book Leverage Ratio")

print(paste("MLR mean: ", mean(MLR2)))
print(paste("BLR mean: ", mean(BLR2)))

summary(data2.noNA$oancf)
summary(data2.noNA$ivncf)
summary(data2.noNA$fincf)
summary(data2.noNA$ebitda)

boxplot(MLR2~data2.noNA$gsector, main = "Market Leverage Ratio")
boxplot(BLR2~data2.noNA$gsector, main = "Book Leverage Ratio")

data2.noNA$MR = MLR2
data2.noNA$BR = BLR2
library(dplyr)
dataIndM = data2.noNA %>% group_by(data2.noNA$gsector) %>% summarise(MR = mean(MR))
dataIndB = data2.noNA %>% group_by(data2.noNA$gsector) %>% summarise(BR = mean(BR))

plot(dataIndM$`data2.noNA$gsector`, dataIndM$MR, main = "Mean Market Leverage Ratio")
plot(dataIndB$`data2.noNA$gsector`, dataIndB$BR, main = "Mean Book Leverage Ratio")

ret = data2.noNA %>% group_by(data2.noNA$gsector) %>% summarise(ebitda = mean(ebitda))
plot(ret$`data2.noNA$gsector`, ret$ebitda, main = "Mean Earning by Industry")
